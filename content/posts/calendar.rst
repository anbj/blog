---
title: "The Calendar - the single point of truth"
date: 2026-05-28T14:43:11+02:00
---

The other day I read that https://basecamp.com/ finally implemented a complete calendar.
In their book, `Shape Up <https://basecamp.com/books#shapeup>`__, they present implementing a proper calendar as not worth the time, though customers requested it.

So I thought to myself, why *did* basecamp develop a calender, despite they at first not wanting to do it?

I thought about it, and it occured to me that a calendar is one of those universally, understood concepts.
Everyone base everything on it.
Here's what I wrote:

*A calendar is like a single point of truth.
Everyone knows it, everyone has it, everyone use it.
You can base basically everything on it.
That's why everyone wants it.*

And then I came to think of it, what other universally SPOT’s is like a calendar?
I would really like to know.
